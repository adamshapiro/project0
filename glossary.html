Glossary

Term
Description
Aerate
Allow air to flow into the milk as it is steamed, causing the milk to expand in volume and create microfoam.
Microfoam
Foam with innumerable air bubbles too small to see individually. Milk used for a latte should have the consistency of wet paint.
Dry
Milk steamed in order to create heavy foam, separated from the milk.
Wet
Milk steamed to create microfoam, without the foam separating.
Au Lait
Steamed milk mixed with drip coffee rather than espresso, which does not need to be aerated.
