Cappuccino

Like a latte, a cappuccino is made by combining espresso with milk steamed to about 160°F. Unlike a latte however, a cappuccino requires the milk to be aerated fairly heavily, resulting in about a doubling in volume and a much greater amount of foam. Depending on the drinker's preference, the amount of foam can be controlled, and the milk and foam may be separated before adding to the espresso.
